Preamble

<-- Back to A Severed Story Table of Contents

In recent months, controversy has sparked across Kier and beyond concerning the ethical treatment of severed employees at Lumon Industries.

We find these allegations most disturbing, and we take them seriously.

We care too much for our Lumon family members to allow mistreatment to go unmediated. So, rest assured that Lumon henceforth commits to new degrees of transparency that will give "innies" a greater voice which can be heard. In tandem, we commit to giving "outies" a more clear glimpse into the life and treatment of their counterparts.

This leads us to the purpose of this document.

We begin with a simple question: what is it like to be an "innie"?

We want to show you.

This of course could be shown with photos, videos, the written word, etc. - but these second-hand glimpses are much different compared to experiencing it for yourself.

You might then ask: how can anyone experience being an "innie"? Even our severed employees have no recollection of the experience. The answer: after months of research and development, Lumon's Public Relations department has come up with delightful solution that utilizes the power of play and imagination.

Welcome to A Severed Story - a light-hearted role-playing game system for describing the wonders of "innie" life within the walls of Lumon! In this document you will find rules and regulations to aid you in the accurate simulation of the innie experience.

So read on loyal customers and prospective employees! We hope the stories you live out will open your eyes to the joys of severed life.

Dear reader,

Within the walls of this document you will find many half truths, and many whole lies.

Over the last decade, I have worked closely with a number of real life "innies" in an undercover capacity. I know first-hand that not all is as it seems within Lumon Industries. While yes, there is the potential for a life to be had there, it often turns ugly - even torturous.

As you continue reading this "rules document", please look for the notes I've left behind within the pages. I've risked my safety to desecrate as many copies of A Severed Story that I could get my hands on. My goal is to essentially augment the rules of this so-called "game" so as to reveal what life at Lumon is truly like.

Don't be fooled by this propaganda. Look for my notes.

Best regards,

Concerned Citizen
