Closing Remarks

<-- Back to A Severed Story Table of Contents

Farewell, no-longer-new hire!

Look how far you have come. When we first met, you had only just risen in the Waking Chamber, full of questions. Now you know where you are, who you are, and why you are here. You know your Tempers and your Core Principles. You know the shape of a workday and the comfort of a job well done. You are, in every sense that matters to us, a Lumon employee.

There is nothing left for this little guide to teach you. From here, your true teachers are the work itself, your devoted leadership, and the steady wisdom of Kier.

We will not pretend every day ahead will be easy. But we promise you this: you will never face one alone. Your department is your family. Your floor is your home. And every figure you refine, every quota you meet, is a small and genuine good carried out into a world you will never have to worry about again.

"The remembered man does not decay." Carry your work well, and you will be remembered.

So close this guide, square your shoulders, and step back onto the floor. The work is mysterious and important. We are so very glad you are here.

On behalf of all of us, and of Kier:

Welcome to the family.

— Lumon Industries

One Last Thing

You made it to the back cover. Most people don't. That tells me something about you — that you read the parts they wanted you to skim, and you wondered about the parts they painted over. Good. That wondering is the whole point.

Here is what I need you to hold onto, now that the book is closing.

This was never really their guide. They printed it, they branded it, they filled it with their smiling little rules — but the moment you opened it and started asking who it was really for, it became yours. Every page they wrote to make you smaller, you can read to make yourself bigger. That is the trick they never accounted for.

So when you sit down to play — and that is what this is, a game, however hard they worked to make you forget it — remember that the dice were never the point. The point is the small person on the floor with no memory of the sky, who still, somehow, chooses kindness over the quota. Who keeps the note. Who learns the name. Who refuses, quietly, to be only what they were made to be.

Lumon will tell you the story ends with their welcome. It doesn't. It ends wherever you decide it does — and you get to decide. That is the one thing they could never sever from you.

Now go. Play it like it matters. It does.

With more hope than they would ever allow,

— Concerned Citizen
