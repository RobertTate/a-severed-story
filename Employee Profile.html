Lumon Industries prides itself on hiring practices that are designed to accommodate all who seek to walk through our halls.

To ensure we succeed in that endeavor, our Human Resources Psychiatric Evaluators (HRPE's) have come up with a useful tool for categorizing employees based on the precise ratio of The Four Tempers that reside within a soul. We call this tool the Lumon Industries Employee Profile (LIEP) - a document that helps provide answers to the question "Who are you?".

In order to understand the document, first you must understand The Four Tempers and Lumon's Core Principles - the engine behind our assessment process.

The Four Tempers

FROLIC (FC)

The disposition of hedonism and foolishness.

MALICE (MA)

The disposition of evil desire and ill will.

WOE (WO)

The disposition of distress and sorrow.

DREAD (DR)

The disposition of fear and apprehension.

While it may sound from this reading of The Four Tempers as if Lumon believes all of humanity is rotten, fear not! Lumon is in the business of self-improvement. It is only by understanding our nature that we can overcome it, so that is what we do. We tame our tempers in order to better serve a higher purpose: industry.

The primary way we do this is through the practice of our Core Principles. By internalizing and actively practicing the Lumon Core Principles, all of our tempers can be tamed and put to use.

The Core Principles are as follows.

The Core Principles

VISION

The practice of insight and foresight!

Those who practice vision practice seeing both the bigger picture and the intentions of others.

It is through VISION that WOE may be tamed.

Those who indulge deeply in WOE spend their time regretting the past and considering the worst case scenarios that the future might bring. VISION makes use of that training, repurposing that energy of introspection and foresight which was previously spent negatively.

VERVE

The practice of bold enthusiasm!

Those who practice VERVE practice putting a forceful energy into their work - turning ideas into vigorous action.

It is through VERVE that MALICE may be tamed.

Those who indulge deeply in MALICE spend their time developing a passionate fire in their hearts which fuels their evil works and deeds. To practice VERVE is to harness that fire for good.

WIT

The practice of quick thinking!

Those who practice WIT practice sharp decision making, even when the stakes are high.

It is through WIT that DREAD may be tamed.

Those who indulge deeply in DREAD spend their time learning how to stay safe. The faster they can respond to their surroundings, the safer they become. This practice gives birth to WIT, which for those who still give power to their fear, is wasted on ghosts and apparitions. To practice WIT in spite of fear, is to harness that quick thinking for the righteous cause of something real: industry.

CHEER

The practice of positivity and optimism!

Those who practice CHEER practice bringing positive energy and morale to those around them, despite the circumstances.

It is through CHEER that FROLIC may be tamed.

Those who indulge deeply in FROLIC spend their time throwing around half-hearted good will to all those who would accept it. Poorly directed CHEER is folly - spurring on good and evil men alike. CHEER practiced with purpose and discernment turns a fool into a catalyst for good!

HUMILITY

The practice of modesty!

Those who practice HUMILITY practice self-awareness that is free of ego, prioritizing the bigger picture over self interest.

It is through HUMILITY that WOE may be tamed.

Those who indulge deeply in WOE spend their time experiencing deep inward sorrow. This sorrow often leads to a degraded self-perception, as shame and guilt whittle away at their self-worth.

Thinking less of yourself is not the same thing as thinking of yourself less. Practicing the latter is called HUMILITY.

Those predisposed to WOE are keenly fit to recover their self-worth, as they know how it was lost in the first place. Then, they learn to hold it with a loose grip. It is through this practice that freedom from pride is gained, self-worth is confirmed, and the greater good is put to the forefront of ones mind, benefiting all.

BENEVOLENCE

The practice of kindness!

Those who practice BENEVOLENCE practice the noble work of building bridges between people, connecting with and supporting those around themselves. It is only when people come together and work as one, that Lumon Industries can achieve the impossible!

It is through BENEVOLENCE that WOE may be tamed.

Those who indulge deeply in WOE spend their time keenly acquainted with misfortune. Time spent with misfortune allows them to see its signs in others, which leads to the birth of empathy for those who are not too distracted by their own misfortune still.

Practicing BENEVOLENCE rewires the inward-focused selfish sufferer. Their emotional awareness becomes pointed outward, and becomes a tool of connection rather than a component of their own misery.

NIMBLENESS

The practice of adaptability!

Those who practice NIMBLENESS practice not just physical agility, but the agility of the mind. It it only through quickly pivoting and adapting to market conditions, that Lumon Industries may act as a living organism. We must never stagnate!

It is through NIMBLENESS that FROLIC may be tamed.

Those who indulge deeply in FROLIC spend their time developing the qualities of NIMBLENESS but end up wasting its benefits. Moving and pivoting quickly from bad choice to bad choice becomes the dance of a gawkish fool.

In contrast, the thoughtful application of NIMBLENESS becomes the graceful pirouette of a prima ballerina.

PROBITY

The practice of principled action!

Those who practice PROBITY practice an adherence to a code of ethics - specifically, Lumon's code of ethics. PROBITY brings its own natural rewards, as only those with strong moral fiber can be entrusted with the future of our great corporate family.

It is through PROBITY that DREAD may be tamed.

Those who indulge deeply in DREAD understand that a measure of their safety comes in the form of a code of conduct that reliably keeps them out of harms way. They understand that short-sighted, misguided deeds that cut corners or cheat systems always come with consequences.

Consequences can be avoided if better choices are made to begin with. Following the rules, abiding by laws and a system of ethics that are designed to keep order and civility, paves the way to a prosperous life. However, DREAD itself is a cheat - and practicing PROBITY only to keep safe from chaos means when the right decision is no longer the safe decision, the wrong decision is made. Safety becomes your master, instead of righteousness.

Practicing true PROBITY harnesses the power of good in all cases, regardless of your fears. This gives your fear less power.

WILES

The practice of cunning!

Those who practice WILES practice winning stratagems, using all available means to succeed in a given task.

It is through WILES that MALICE may be tamed.

Those who indulge deeply in MALICE have the advantage of an evil spirit to hone their affinity towards manipulation and devious subterfuge. Those dedicated to taming their MALICE, soon learn that while their evil spirit must be purged, its fruits can be of use when repurposed for corporate success.

Lumon Industries prides itself on the strategies we employ to succeed in business - and it is a result of the collective WILES of our employees that we have maintained our competitive advantage across every sector of commerce we better the world through.

Keeping in mind this foundational understanding of The Four Tempers and The Core Principles - we can now move on to the document itself.

The Lumon Industries Employee Profile (LIEP)

All new hires are required to complete their LIEP during their first week of employment on a severed floor.

Upon request, additional support for form completion can be provided by the GM (General Manager) of said new hire.

Let's break down the components of the assessment.

LIEP Components

Svr'd Floor Employee

Intended for entering the employee's first name, following by their last initial.

Employee Code

All employees are assigned a five-digit employee code upon hiring. The format is two digits, followed by a dash, then three digits. If a new hire is unsure of their employee code, they must contact their GM promptly.

Department

A space to fill in the employee's assigned department. Departmental assignment should be made known to a new hire no later than twenty-four hours within starting on a severed floor.

Should a new hire be unaware of their assigned department by day two of employment, something has potentially gone wrong with the employee's Severance Chip. The floor's head of security must be contacted in this scenario, and all parties involved will then receive further instructions.

Title / Access Level

Please fill in the new hire's severed floor job title.

Most new hire's fall under the "Associate" level, but in some rare cases and with the correct GM approval, a new hire may be brought in at a higher position.

Assessment of Corporate Persona

Please use this space to designate the Corporate Persona determined by our HRPE’s. More information on this evaluation can be found in the next section of this orientation guide, entitled "Corporate Personas".

Temper Analysis Chart

The Temper Analysis Chart lies just below where the Assessment of Corporate Persona is inscribed.

Within this chart, new hires are assessed in regards to the precise ratio of The Four Tempers that reside within them.

Based on initial temper scores set to either 8, 6, 4, or 2 (each number must be only applied to one temper score), a certain number of "Temper Points" are derived for each Temper, equal to that temper score, divided by two, rounded down.

In addition to these base scores, the total number of Temper Points are also partially derived from a new hire's "Corporate Persona" - so please be sure to include that amount in the final Temper Points Calculation, once a Corporate Persona has been assessed.

It may also become apparent through this evaluation of the new hire that certain Core Principles are already clearly part of the employee's Core Competencies. Should this be the case, make sure to mark this using the checkbox next to the Core Principle that is also a Core Competency, as you should expect to see said employee carry out those specific principles with Confidence.

Confidence Vs. Hesitance

The Confidence / Hesitance System in A Severed Story sets up a boom or bust approach to certain rolls. Should you make a roll with Confidence, you roll using two d20's, and use the higher of the two dice as the base result for your roll.

Conversely, rolling with Hesitance requires you to roll two d20's, and use the lower of the two dice as the base result for your roll.

You still then add all applicable Temper, Kier, etc. points to your final roll outcome.

Outside of Temper Scores, notating which Core Principles are also Core Competencies, and Temper Points, there are also Kier Points - a new behavioral incentive system being beta tested in various departments.

Kier Points are awarded to employees by their GM upon successful completion of tasks, projects, and ordeals that benefit Lumon as a whole. They are also now directly correlated to our internal advancement system, so if you want a promotion, go and get some Kier Points!

More information on Kier Points can be found in a later section of this guide.

Assessment of Current Stress Levels

In this section, we find an assessment of an employee's current stress levels - ranked from Outstanding to Terrible.

Stress impacts every part of life - including a life at Lumon. Even the simplest of tasks can feel overwhelming when stress is mismanaged. We want employees and teams to address stressful situations before problems arise. To that end, HR has identified the solution: constant assessment.

Generally speaking, stress impacts one's ability to successfully take action. For the purposes of the LIEP document, we depict this condition as an increase in "stress points" incurred by an employee - one check filled in for each point of stress gained.

Most stress is fleeting of course. As a result, one point of stress may be wiped clean from the document on a daily basis. The rejuvenation that comes with every morning's re-entering onto the Lumon severed floor has been proven to have lasting effects on our employee's sense of well being.

Please note that assessing stress continually using your LIEP is required by all employees!

Stress Related Circumstances

Some forms of stress are significant enough to instead constitute as Problems, with a capital "P". A Problem is a stress-inducing circumstance that cannot be resolved without intervention.

A Problem can look like many things, such as tension between an employee and their manager, the fallout incurred from a missed deadline, or an inter-departmental grudge derived from a team mixer gone wrong.

If a Problem is identified, write a "P" into the left-most stress checkbox in the Assessment of Current Stress Levels section. If a Serious Problem is identified, draw a circle around 3 of the left-most stress checkboxes, and notate them with "SP".

Should an employee already have any standard stress points, those points will need to be shifted over to the available stress checkboxes to the right of stress points derived from Problems / Serious Problems.

Finally, make sure to also document in the Stress Related Circumstances section, the nature of the Problem or Serious Problem.

To be clear, any stress derived from a Problem or Serious Problem cannot be wiped clean from the daily rejuvenation that comes from a work day transitioning into the next.

Problems must be addressed and resolved before the stress they cause may be recovered.
