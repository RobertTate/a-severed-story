Performance Evaluation

<-- Back to A Severed Story Table of Contents

(Character Advancement)

An employee's career on the severed floor is measured by their accumulating commitment to Lumon's values. We call this measurement Kier Points. As an employee accrues them, they ascend through a series of tiers. Kiers Tiers each unlock new responsibilities, deeper access, and the trust of those above.

And for those of us paying attention, there is a parallel system — Ricken Points — that measures your journey away from Lumon's stranglehold and toward yourself. Same shape. Opposite content. Both Tracks can be walked at once. The bravest employees walk them in step.

— C.C.

Earning Points

There is no fixed schedule for the awarding of Kier or Ricken Points. They are awarded by the GM at moments of narrative significance, when the GM judges that an employee's conduct has meaningfully advanced — or undermined — Lumon's interests.

The earning of a Point is a story beat, not a bookkeeping action. The GM is encouraged to make the moment felt.

  • The floor manager pulls the employee aside after a meeting to praise their dedication.

  • A promotion is announced at the next team mixer.

  • A loose page from a forbidden book is found tucked inside a desk drawer.

  • A coworker quietly shares a rumor that was never theirs to share.

Where the Point Goes

Every Kier or Ricken Point is placed into a specific row of the Temper Analysis Chart on the LIEP — the row whose Temper drove the qualifying action.

An employee who succeeded at a tense meeting by exercising sharp WIT (parent Temper: DREAD) receives their Point in the DREAD row. An employee who soothed a tense moment with practiced CHEER (parent Temper: FROLIC) receives theirs in the FROLIC row. When more than one Temper might apply, the GM's call is final.

Why row placement matters.

Points placed in a row are added to that row's Core Principle Rolls — see The Roll in Procedural Guidelines. Row placement therefore turns roleplay choices into mechanical specialization. The shape of an employee's career is the shape of their character sheet.

Determining Tier

An employee's current Tier is determined by the total number of Kier or Ricken Points they hold, summed across all four Temper rows.

  • 1 – 2 Points — Tier I

  • 3 – 4 Points — Tier II

  • 5 – 6 Points — Tier III

  • 7 – 8 Points — Tier IV

  • 9 – 10 Points — Tier V

Each Tier brings with it a set of Boons — narrative permissions, mechanical privileges, and (for the Ricken Track) Persistent Contraband. Boons persist for as long as the employee holds the points that earned them. Should the employee drop a Tier, the Boons drop with them.

The Kier Track

The Corporate Path.

Lumon recognizes those who exemplify our values. The Kier Track is that recognition made visible.

Tier I — Associate

A standard employee in good standing.

Boons

  • Basic departmental access.

  • Eligible for standard employee benefits.

Tier II — Senior Associate

A proven and dependable employee.

Boons

  • Access to personnel records.

  • Trusted with sensitive departmental information.

Tier III — Distinguished Associate

An employee recognized for exceptional performance.

Boons

  • Access to restricted departmental resources.

  • May request special corporate accommodations.

Tier IV — Chief Associate

The highest-ranking associate within the department.

Restrictions

  • Only one employee may hold this title at a time. Should two employees qualify, the GM may stage an internal competition, or arbitrate the placement narratively.

Boons

  • Access to department-wide records.

  • May sponsor privileges for other employees.

  • Considered the primary employee representative of the department.

Tier V — Department Chief

The official leader of the department.

Restrictions

  • Only one employee may hold this title at a time.

Boons

  • Full departmental access.

  • Access to executive communications.

  • Authority to authorize departmental initiatives.

  • Direct communication with management.

Kier Philosophy

Kier advancement grants official power — recognition, trust, access, responsibility, and authority. The Kier Track rewards those who excel within the system.

Dear reader,

If you've read this far, you've earned the rest. What follows is the resistance's mirror to the Kier Track — what I call the Ricken Track.

All the rules above apply: Points are earned in narrative moments, placed in rows by the Temper that drove the act, and summed for Tier. Where Kier rewards loyalty with badges, Ricken rewards curiosity with knowledge and Contraband. Where a Kier Tier comes with a manager's handshake, a Ricken Tier comes with a stranger's voice in the dark.

One warning. Lumon does not love its critics. Walking this Track is more dangerous than walking Kier's — and walking both at once is dangerous in proportion to how far you have come on each.

Read on.

— C.C.

The Ricken Track

The Resistance Path.

Tier I — Reader

You've found something you weren't meant to read.

Boons

  • Learn a secret rumor from the GM.

  • Gain access to a hidden stash location.

Persistent Contraband

  • A hidden note, page, or document.

Tier II — Correspondent

You've established contact beyond your department.

Boons

  • Access to interdepartmental information.

  • Ability to receive hidden messages.

Persistent Contraband

  • Unauthorized keycard, or a hidden communication channel.

Tier III — Conspirator

You've become part of an underground network.

Boons

  • Access to restricted employee information.

  • May call upon underground contacts for assistance.

Persistent Contraband

  • Unauthorized department maps, or a record of hidden routes.

Tier IV — Agitator

Your actions actively influence the resistance.

Boons

  • Access to multiple underground contacts.

  • Ability to establish safe locations.

  • Ability to conceal evidence from management.

  • Once per workday, temporarily suppress the effects of a Problem.

Persistent Contraband

  • A secret contact list, or the address of a hidden meeting location.

Tier V — Awakener

You possess knowledge capable of threatening Lumon itself.

Boons

  • Ability to establish underground cells.

  • Ability to reveal hidden truths to other employees.

  • Once per workday, gain Confidence on any action involving Lumon secrets.

Persistent Contraband

  • Forbidden archives, or a high-security stolen resource.

Ricken Philosophy

Ricken advancement grants unofficial power — secrets, connections, leverage, and knowledge. The Ricken Track rewards those who look beyond the system.

Contraband

Contraband is the physical and informational currency of the resistance. It comes in two forms.

Persistent Contraband

Persistent Contraband is granted by the Ricken Track at each new Tier. As long as the employee holds the Tier, they hold the Contraband.

When a Roll would be meaningfully aided by a relevant piece of Persistent Contraband, the GM may choose one of:

  • Waive the Roll entirely — the action just succeeds. (You have the keycard. The door opens.)

  • Reduce the Roll's Task Severity by one tier — Arduous becomes Demanding, Demanding becomes Routine, and so on.

  • Grant Confidence on the Roll.

If an employee drops a Tier, the GM decides which piece of Persistent Contraband is lost — and how. A keycard demagnetizes. A map is discovered by Security. A contact stops returning calls. The world finds a way.

Consumable Contraband

Consumable Contraband exists in the world independent of any Track. Any employee may find Consumable Contraband — by stumbling across it, by being given it, or by deliberately seeking it out. The GM controls availability.

A Consumable item is spent for a guaranteed Success on a single related Roll, or to bypass a single specific obstacle. Examples include a smuggled note containing a date or password, a smuggled snack that restores one stress, or a single-charge access token.

Discovery has consequences.

Persistent or Consumable, Contraband discovered by Lumon is confiscated. The GM may also impose a Demerit, a Kier Point loss, or both — depending on how visibly the employee was caught.

Two Paths, Shared Destinations

The Kier and Ricken Tracks mirror one another. At equivalent Tiers, they often grant access to the same destinations — just through different doors.

  • Official Keycard  ↔  Stolen Keycard

  • Department Records  ↔  Secret Records

  • Promotion  ↔  Conspiracy

  • Authority  ↔  Leverage

  • Resources  ↔  Secrets

  • Trust  ↔  Knowledge

A Tier V Kier employee and a Tier V Ricken employee may ultimately stand in the same room. The Kier employee walks through the front door, because they are authorized. The Ricken employee finds a way in, because they discovered a route nobody else knows exists.

Pursuing Both Tracks

Nothing in this guide prevents an employee from advancing on both Tracks at once. Some of the most consequential careers at Lumon have done so. But the further an employee has come down one path, the harder the other becomes.

When an employee makes a play for Points on a Track where the other Track is already developed, the GM may:

  • Raise the Task Severity of any Roll involved.

  • Require an explicit Roll where one would otherwise be waived.

  • Introduce opportunities for discovery — Lumon eyes notice contradictions. The resistance notices fence-sitters.

These are GM judgments, made in the spirit of the moment.

Losing Points

Points may be lost as well as earned. Loss is also a story beat — driven by what occurred, and how it was witnessed.

Kier Points are typically lost when an employee is caught working against Lumon's interests: a discovered piece of Contraband, a meeting overheard, a record found altered.

Ricken Points are typically lost when an employee is seen by Ricken-aligned allies as too cooperative with Lumon: accepting a promotion publicly, naming a coworker, reporting a rumor. Trust is the resistance's currency, and like any currency it can be spent away.

The GM decides how many Points are lost and from which row — driven by the moment and what occurred. When losses drop an employee below a Tier threshold, the Tier drops with them, along with all of that Tier's Boons and, for the Ricken Track, the corresponding Persistent Contraband.

Demerits and Termination

Demerits are tracked separately from Kier Points. They accumulate toward Termination (see Burnout in Procedural Guidelines).

A Demerit is most commonly issued as a consequence of Burnout. However, the GM may issue an additional Demerit at any time if an employee has done something the GM judges unforgivable in Lumon's eyes — even if Burnout has not occurred.

Three Demerits remains the threshold for Termination. Kier Point loss may accompany a Demerit; it does not replace one.

A Note on "Winning"

There is no fixed win condition for A Severed Story. Reaching Tier V on either Track is not the end of the game any more than reaching the highest level of any other tabletop role-playing system is the end of a campaign.

The Tier system equips the employee. The campaign is the story the GM tells with them, and the ending is the story's ending — not the sheet's.
