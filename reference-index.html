(An Appendix of Terms)

A quick reference for the terms used throughout A Severed Story. Each entry points back to the chapter where it is taught in full — follow it there for the complete treatment.

Glossary

A Severed Story. This game — a handbook of life and joyful labor on a Lumon severed floor, and the rules for playing it. See the Preamble.

Boon. A standing benefit earned upon reaching a new Tier in a row. See Performance Evaluation.

Burnout. What befalls an employee whose stress fills every box. Burnout earns a Demerit. See Procedural Guidelines.

Confidence. A favorable Roll: throw 2d20 and take the higher die. Granted when a Roll falls under one of an employee's Core Competencies. See Procedural Guidelines.

Core Competency. A Core Principle an employee is especially practiced in, marked on the LIEP. Rolls made under it are made with Confidence. See Employee Profile.

Core Principle. One of the Nine practiced virtues of Lumon. Every Roll is made under the Principle that best embodies the action. See Employee Profile.

Corporate Persona. An archetype assigned by the LIEP that shapes an employee's Temper Points and grants Special Attributes. See Corporate Personas.

d20. The twenty-sided die. The heart of every Roll. See Procedural Guidelines.

Demerit. A formal mark against an employee. Three Demerits bring Termination. See Procedural Guidelines.

Departmental Standing. A department's collective reputation, which rises and falls with the conduct of all its members. See Classified.

The Four Tempers. Frolic, Malice, Woe, and Dread — the four dispositions of the soul. Each Core Principle answers to, and tames, a parent Temper. See Employee Profile.

GM. Your General Manager, who narrates the floor, sets each Task Severity, and adjudicates every Roll. (Some tables read the letters differently. Both readings are correct.) See A Severed Story.

Hesitance. An unfavorable Roll: throw 2d20 and take the lower die. Imposed by mounting stress and other burdens. See Procedural Guidelines.

Kier Point. A point earned for service to Lumon, recorded in a Temper row and summed toward that row's rolls. See Performance Evaluation.

LIEP. The Lumon Industries Employee Profile — the document that records who an employee is, and the sheet you play from. See Employee Profile.

Modifier. Any number added to a Roll before it is compared to Task Severity — chiefly Temper Points and Kier Points. See Procedural Guidelines.

(And Ricken Points.)

— C.C.

Outcome. The result of a Roll: Critical Success (a natural 20), Success (meets or beats the Task Severity), Failure (falls short), or Critical Failure (a natural 1). See Procedural Guidelines.

Problem. A lingering complication, often born of a Critical Failure. Its stress will not clear at the end of the workday until it is resolved through play. A Serious Problem is a graver, more stubborn one. See Procedural Guidelines.

Roll. The core action of play: a d20, plus Modifiers, measured against a Task Severity. See Procedural Guidelines.

Session. A single sitting of play, of any number of workdays. See Day to Day Operations.

Stress. An employee's mounting strain, tracked across ten boxes on the LIEP. It descends — clears — at the end of each workday, save where a Problem holds it fast. See Procedural Guidelines.

Stress Range. The five bands of the stress track, from rest to ruin: Outstanding, Satisfactory, Tolerable, Poor, and Terrible. See Procedural Guidelines.

Task Severity. The number a Roll must meet or beat, set by the GM: Routine (10), Demanding (15), Arduous (20), or Heroic (25). See Procedural Guidelines.

Temper Point. A Modifier drawn from a Temper row, equal to that Temper's score halved (rounded down), plus any granted by an employee's Corporate Persona. See Employee Profile.

Termination. The end of an employee's tenure, brought on by a third Demerit. See Procedural Guidelines.

Tier. A rank from I to V, reached by accumulating Points in a row. Each new Tier confers a Boon. See Performance Evaluation.

Wellness Session. A restorative session that relieves an employee's accumulated stress. See Day to Day Operations.

Workday. The basic in-world unit of play. One box of stress is removed at the start of each workday. See Day to Day Operations.

Terms They Left Out

Here are the terms the handbook won't print.

Ricken Point. The mirror of a Kier Point. You earn it the same way — through a row, toward the same Tiers — but for an act that serves you, or the person beside you, instead of Lumon. The book hands you one ledger. You always had two — I set the second one down beside theirs in Performance Evaluation.

Contraband. Anything Lumon did not issue you and cannot account for. Persistent contraband is a thing you keep and hide; Consumable contraband is spent in a single, precious use. A note, a map, a name. Hold onto it — I catalogued the kinds worth keeping in Performance Evaluation.

The Break Room. Not a break. Ask anyone who came back from one quieter than they went in. The handbook will not define this for you — but I told the truth of it in the Classified pages.

Burnout (the true entry). They print it as your failure — your stress, your weakness, your box filled up. It is theirs. No one was ever meant to be worn down to nothing for a quota.

Termination (the true entry). For an outie, a lost job. For an innie, there is no severance package, no next thing, no door home. There is only the end of you.

— Concerned Citizen
